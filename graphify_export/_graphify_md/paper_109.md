# Bourgon V, Duval F-G, Forest G. Circadian athletic variations when traveling in the Canadian Football League. Chronobiology International. 2026. DOI: 10.1080/07420528.2026.2637795

## TLDR
CFL teams traveling westward for evening games perform significantly worse than those traveling eastward or within the same time zone. Defensive variables were most sensitive to circadian disruption from westward travel, while offensive variables were largely unaffected except total first downs. Performance decline scales with longitudinal distance traveled westward. Staff should prioritize circadian management strategies — light exposure, sleep scheduling, and travel planning — especially when teams face long westward trips to evening games.

## Abstract
This study investigated circadian disadvantage related to travel direction and longitudinal distance in the Canadian Football League (CFL) using 8 years (2014–2021) of regular season evening game data (n=387 away games). One-way ANOVAs and linear regression analyses were applied across three travel direction categories (westward, same time zone, eastward) for team, offensive, and defensive performance variables. Results showed that westward travel was associated with worse score differentials and time of possession, and that greater longitudinal distance traveled westward predicted poorer defensive performance, while eastward travel was associated with performance improvements.

## Methods
Retrospective analysis of 387 CFL regular season away evening games (after 18:00 local time) from 2014 to 2021 (pre-COVID). Data extracted from footballdb.com and cfl.com. Variables categorized as team (score differential, time of possession, penalties), offensive (first downs, yards per play, pass completion, interceptions, sacks, fumbles, kickoff/punt returns, field goal %), and defensive (same metrics allowed). Travel direction (westward, same time zone, eastward) and longitudinal distance traveled were the independent variables. One-way ANOVAs with LSD post hoc t-tests and linear regression analyses were conducted.

## Findings
Significant effects of travel direction on score differential (F[2,286]=3.86, p=0.022, η²=.06) and time of possession (F[2,286]=5.42, p=0.005, η²=.09). Post hoc tests confirmed westward travelers performed worse on both variables. Defensive variables significantly affected by travel direction included average yards allowed per play (p=0.008), average yards allowed per passing play (p=0.005), and average kickoff return yards allowed (p=0.035). Regression analyses confirmed that greater longitudinal distance traveled westward significantly predicted worse score differential (β=.13, p<.001), less time of possession (β=2.06, p<.001), fewer first downs made (β=.03, p=.004), and worse defensive metrics including yards allowed per play (β=-.01, p<.001) and first downs allowed (β=-.04, p=.003).

## Limitations
Data were observational and derived from publicly available sources, limiting causal inference. Circadian phase was inferred from travel direction and time zone crossings rather than direct biological measures (melatonin, core body temperature, actigraphy). Performance metrics were assessed at the team level, potentially masking individual variability. Travel duration, sleep quality, injuries, and lineup changes were not controlled. Data limited to 2014–2021.

## Practical Implications
NFL and CFL teams should incorporate structured circadian management protocols for westward-traveling teams playing evening games. Interventions such as controlled light exposure, strategic sleep scheduling, phase-advancing strategies, and careful travel planning can help mitigate circadian disadvantages. The finding that eastward travel may provide a circadian advantage suggests scheduling considerations for game times and travel logistics could offer a meaningful competitive edge.

## Athlete Development Notes
Player development programs should educate athletes on chronobiology and circadian rhythm management as a performance factor. The differential impact on defensive versus offensive play suggests reactive skill development (defensive reads, pass coverage, decision speed) may be especially vulnerable to circadian disruption, making circadian optimization strategies particularly important for defensive players during road trips.

## Return to Play Notes
Return-to-play protocols for CFL and NFL athletes should account for circadian disruption from travel, particularly westward travel for evening games. Clinicians assessing readiness should consider that reactive, cognitively demanding tasks (pass coverage, pursuit angles) may be more impaired by travel fatigue than pre-planned offensive sequences. Scheduling return-to-participation assessments at the same time of day as upcoming games helps ensure accurate functional readiness evaluation.
