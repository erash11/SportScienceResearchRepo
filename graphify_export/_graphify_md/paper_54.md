# Mack CD, Wasserman EB, Anderson DJ, Farkas G, Delaney M, Eichner D, Johnston K, Lassiter MK, Myers E, Mayer T, Solomon G, Sills A. Anatomy of an American football game: Player to player contact before, during and after an NFL game in context of the 2020 COVID-19 pandemic. The Physician and Sportsmedicine. 2022;doi:10.1080/00913847.2022.2028536.

## TLDR
NFL players have limited and brief cross-team contact during game day; most interactions are under 5 consecutive minutes, suggesting that a robust off-field prevention program can effectively reduce infectious disease transmission risk in professional football.

## Abstract
This study quantified player-to-player contact exposure in all 256 NFL regular season games during the 2020 season using Kinexon wearable proximity tracking devices. Cross-Club consecutive interactions of 2 or more minutes within 6 feet were measured pre-game, in-game, and post-game. Of 1,964 distinct player-to-opponent contacts lasting at least 2 minutes, the majority (87%) were fewer than 5 consecutive minutes. A cumulative analysis of a representative subset of 119 games found an average of 17.1 player-opponent interactions with cumulative exposure of at least 5 minutes per game, nearly all occurring during competition.

## Methods
Observational study of all 256 NFL 2020 regular season games (September 10, 2020–January 3, 2021). Kinexon proximity tracking devices (worn by all Club personnel) measured consecutive and cumulative interactions ≥2 minutes within 6 feet between opposing players. Game schedules and rosters from the NFL GSIS were combined with proximity data. Contacts were smoothed using Maximum Distance (1.8 m), Maximum Sequence Gap (15 s), and Minimum Consecutive Duration (120 s) parameters. A 90-minute pre-game to 30-minute post-game window was applied. Descriptive statistics (mean, median, IQR) and Poisson models were used; chi-squared tests compared positional groups.

## Findings
• 1,964 distinct player-to-opponent contacts ≥2 minutes identified across 256 games
• 87% (n=1,699) of consecutive contacts were <5 minutes in length
• Mean 7.7 distinct contacts ≥2 minutes per player per game (median=4; IQR=2,8)
• Very few contacts reached ≥5 consecutive minutes (mean=1.0; median=0)
• 43.2% of contacts occurred pre-game, 27.8% during competition, 29% post-game
• Cumulative analysis (n=119 games): mean 17.1 player-opponent interactions ≥5 min cumulative exposure, almost all during competition
• Offensive and defensive linemen had the highest proportion of contacts ≥5 consecutive minutes

## Limitations
Kinexon devices did not provide geolocation (field vs. sideline vs. locker room), so contact setting could not be confirmed. The 90-minute game window was empirically defined and may have excluded some legitimate interactions. Cumulative interaction analysis was only feasible for a subset of 119 games due to data size constraints. The analysis focused solely on game day; practice and travel contacts were excluded.

## Practical Implications
Game-day contact between opposing NFL players is brief and infrequent, meaning off-field environments (travel, facilities) are likely the greater infectious disease transmission risk. A comprehensive prevention program integrating masking, distancing, hygiene, and ventilation off the field can substantially reduce overall exposure risk. Contact tracing efforts should prioritize pre- and post-game social interactions and indoor facility settings.

## Athlete Development Notes
Directly applicable to American football (NFL). Data confirm that linemen accumulate the most prolonged opponent contact, which has implications for targeted health monitoring and infection-control protocols within positional groups. Understanding game-day contact patterns can also inform load and recovery planning for high-contact positions.

## Return to Play Notes
Not directly focused on return-to-play; however, the study's framework for tracking player proximity and exposure duration using wearables has direct relevance to monitoring athlete availability and contact load during return-to-play progressions following illness or quarantine in the NFL setting.
