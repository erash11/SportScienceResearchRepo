# Knapik DM, Mack CD, Lee R, Heersink W, Tanenbaum JE, Wetzel RJ, Voos JE. Epidemiology of Tibial Fractures in Professional American Football Athletes From 2013 to 2019. Orthop J Sports Med. 2021;9(11):23259671211040878. doi:10.1177/23259671211040878

## TLDR
Over seven NFL seasons, tibial fractures were uncommon but highly consequential, causing a median of 74 days lost and up to nearly a full year for surgically managed cases. Defensive backs and running backs were most affected, with most fractures occurring during the final four weeks of the regular season via direct contact during tackling. This late-season clustering points to fatigue-related neuromuscular breakdown as a likely contributor, underscoring the value of load management and neuromuscular maintenance late in the season.

## Abstract
This retrospective case series reviewed the NFL injury database (ISS 2013-2014; EHR 2015-2019) to identify all tibial fractures over seven seasons. A total of 64 fractures in 60 athletes were identified. Median time lost was 74 days. Defensive secondary athletes had the highest proportion (16%), followed by running backs (14%). The majority (61%) occurred during regular-season games, predominantly in the final 4 weeks (weeks 13-17). Being tackled with a direct impact to the leg was the most common mechanism. Lateral tibial plateau fractures were most frequent; distal tibial fractures caused the greatest time loss (mean 175 days). Surgery was required in 48% of EHR-era injuries; surgical cases missed a median of 232 days versus 56 days for conservatively managed cases.

## Methods
Retrospective case series using the NFL Injury Surveillance System (ISS) for 2013-2014 and the centralized electronic health record (EHR) system for 2015-2019. All tibial fractures were identified; purely ligamentous injuries and bony contusions without definite fracture were excluded. Variables collected included athlete characteristics, injury timing, fracture type and characteristics, associated injuries, management (operative vs conservative), and days missed. Surgical procedures were identified via CPT codes reviewed by an IQVIA analytics team. Descriptive statistics were calculated with SPSS v23.

## Findings
64 tibial fractures in 60 athletes; median days lost 74 (IQR 34-153). Defensive secondary (16%) and running backs (14%) had highest injury counts. 61% occurred during regular-season games; 51% of those in weeks 13-17. Most common activity: being tackled (27%); most common mechanism: direct impact (58%). Lateral tibial plateau fractures were most common (46%); distal tibial fractures caused greatest time loss (mean 175 days). 48% of EHR-era injuries required surgery; surgical cases lost median 232 days vs 56 days for conservative management. Open fractures occurred in 4%; intra-articular involvement in 43% of distal tibial fractures.

## Limitations
Retrospective design depends on completeness of NFL ISS and EHR documentation. Data quality improved after EHR launch, so 2013-2014 season data (ISS) were excluded from surgical and RTP analyses. Days missed was unavailable in 36% of injuries. Data on athletes who left the NFL after fracture were not collected. Overall incidence rates by position exposure were not calculated. Mechanism and activity data were entered by athletic trainers without systematic video validation. Imaging and operative reports were not available for review. Results are specific to professional football and may not generalize to other sports or competition levels.

## Practical Implications
Medical staff should have high suspicion for concomitant ligamentous and meniscal injuries in athletes with lateral tibial plateau fractures given the 29% co-injury rate. For fractures amenable to intramedullary nailing, earlier return-to-sport can be anticipated versus those requiring ORIF. Late-season fatigue-related injury clustering suggests value in monitoring neuromuscular readiness in the final weeks of the season to reduce severe lower extremity injury risk.

## Athlete Development Notes
Defensive secondary and running back athletes face the highest tibial fracture risk, reflecting the open-field tackling demands of these positions. Position-specific conditioning that emphasizes neuromuscular control, dynamic balance, and proper tackling mechanics — especially during the high-fatigue late-season period — may reduce fracture risk. Monitoring cumulative load and incorporating deload strategies in weeks 12-17 may be warranted.

## Return to Play Notes
Tibial fractures carry significant RTP timelines: median 74 days overall, 232 days for surgical cases. Intramedullary nailing has demonstrated 90% RTP rates with longer career longevity than ORIF. Distal tibial fractures with intra-articular extension and concomitant fibular fracture carry the longest RTP timeline. Staged functional rehabilitation with clearance criteria for full sport participation is critical given the high rate of surgical intervention in this population.
