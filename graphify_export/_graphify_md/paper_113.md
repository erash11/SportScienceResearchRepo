# Assef N, Endsley C, Skoumal C, Schlafke J, Dewald M. Closed Kinetic Chain Upper Extremity Stability Test Reference Values for NCAA American Football. International Journal of Sports Physical Therapy. 2025;20(9):1338-1344. doi:10.26603/001c.143374

## TLDR
NCAA American football players averaged 27 touches on the CKCUEST, with a position-based distinction: linemen score significantly lower (~25.7) than all other positions (~28.1). A change of at least 2 touches represents a clinically meaningful difference. Prior upper extremity injury did not reduce scores, supporting use of this test as an objective RTP benchmark regardless of injury history. Staff should apply position-specific reference values rather than a single universal cutoff when using the CKCUEST for return-to-sport clearance in football.

## Abstract
This cross-sectional study established CKCUEST reference values for 80 male NCAA football players (Divisions 1, 2, and 3) from three institutions, accounting for position, injury history, year in school, and division. Players averaged 27.3 touches with a minimal detectable change of 1.2 touches (clinically, 2 touches). Linemen (offensive and defensive combined) averaged significantly fewer touches (25.7) than all other positions combined (28.1, p<0.01), and linebackers averaged more touches (29.7) than offensive linemen (24.8, p=0.03). No significant differences were found based on NCAA division, year in school, or history of upper extremity injury.

## Methods
Cross-sectional study approved by three university IRBs with data collected spring/summer 2024. Eighty male NCAA football players (Division 1 n=21, Division 2 n=37, Division 3 n=22) completed three 15-second CKCUEST trials (push-up position, hands 36 inches apart, alternating hand touches for maximum repetitions) with 45-second rest between trials; trials were averaged for the score. Independent variables: position, height, mass, BMI, year in school, upper extremity injury history. Comparisons used one-way ANOVA with Bonferroni post hoc corrections, independent t-tests for linemen vs. all other positions, and Pearson correlation coefficients for continuous anthropometric variables. MDC95 calculated from SEM.

## Findings
Mean touches: 27.3 (95% CI 26.4–28.1). MDC: 1.2 touches (2 touches needed for clinically meaningful change). Linebackers (29.7) vs. offensive linemen (24.8): p=0.03. Linemen as a group (25.7) vs. all other positions (28.1): p<0.01. No significant correlations between touches and height (r=−0.02), mass (r=−0.18), BMI (r=−0.20), year in school (r=0.16), or division (r=0.12). No significant difference between players with (26.6) and without (27.6) upper extremity injury history (p=0.30). No significant differences by NCAA division (Division 1: 26.0, Division 2: 28.0, Division 3: 27.2).

## Limitations
Data collected at three sites with different researchers, introducing potential inter-rater variability (mitigated by standardized video instructions and phone follow-up). Different floor surfaces across facilities may have influenced touch counts. Some positions had small sample sizes, limiting statistical power for pairwise position comparisons. Injury history was self-reported and not differentiated by injury type or severity.

## Practical Implications
A reference value of 27 touches should be used as a benchmark for NCAA football players on the CKCUEST. A change of at least 2 touches represents a clinically meaningful difference. Position-specific norms should be applied: linemen (OL and DL) typically score around 25–26 touches, while non-lineman positions average 28+ touches. Staff should not use a single universal benchmark for return-to-sport clearance without accounting for positional demands. Pre-season CKCUEST scores below 21 touches have been previously associated with elevated in-season shoulder injury risk.

## Athlete Development Notes
The CKCUEST is a practical, low-equipment upper extremity functional assessment suitable for pre-season screening of all football position groups. Establishing position-specific baseline scores at the start of each season allows staff to track changes in upper extremity stability and identify at-risk athletes. Linemen's lower normative scores (25.7) reflect their unique positional demands and higher body mass, and development programs for linemen should incorporate closed-chain upper extremity strength and stability training to optimize performance on this metric.

## Return to Play Notes
The CKCUEST with a football-specific reference value of 27 touches (or position-adjusted: ~25.7 for linemen, ~28.1 for non-linemen) provides a standardized, validated benchmark for upper extremity return-to-sport clearance following shoulder, elbow, or wrist injuries in NCAA and NFL-level football players. A minimum of 2 touches improvement from post-injury baseline is needed to confirm measurable recovery. Prior upper extremity injury history did not reduce CKCUEST performance, supporting its use as an objective clearance criterion regardless of injury background.
