# Copeland A, Heinze KL. An examination of competitive rule evolution in the National Football League. Managing Sport and Leisure. 2024; DOI: 10.1080/23750472.2024.2369323.

## TLDR
NFL player conduct rules generally expanded in scope and length from 1980 to 2020, driven by institutional pressures around player safety, officiating quality, and competitive fairness, offering a framework for understanding how sport rules evolve incrementally over time.

## Abstract
This study examined how competitive rules evolve in professional sport using a qualitative document analysis of NFL rule books published between 1980 and 2020. The authors focused on player conduct rules—specifically unsportsmanlike conduct, unnecessary roughness, roughing the passer, chop blocking, crackback blocking, and clipping—to trace longitudinal trajectories of rule change. An inductive coding approach identified rule components (actions, actors, times, spaces, sanctions) and change types (expansion, elaboration, reduction, deletion). Findings were contextualized using NFL public communications to connect rule changes to institutional factors.

## Methods
Qualitative document analysis (QDA) of 35 NFL rule books (1980–2020); Microsoft Word 'compare documents' feature used to identify text changes at five-year intervals; inductive coding in NVivo to identify rule components and change types; analyst triangulation; trajectories constructed for six player conduct rules.

## Findings
• Rule changes generally expanded the set of prohibited actions and the number of actors protected or prohibited. • Rules applied to more game moments (times) and more field/corporeal spaces over time. • Sanctions for rule violations tended to increase in severity. • Rules followed an expansion-elaboration trajectory with a mid-period reduction phase before expanding again by 2020. • Rule word count increased substantially across the observation period. • Institutional pressures—player safety, officiating quality, league legitimacy—appeared to drive most changes.

## Limitations
Rule books from six seasons (1990, 1991, 2004, 2005, 2007, 2008) were missing and substituted with adjacent years; qualitative approach limits generalizability; only six rules were analyzed; focus on player conduct rules excludes scoring and other game mechanics rules; study does not measure outcomes of rule changes.

## Practical Implications
Sport organizations and governing bodies can use this framework to track, anticipate, and justify rule changes by examining changes at the component level. Leaders should communicate rule changes in terms of their institutional rationale (safety, fairness, entertainment) to improve player, coach, and official buy-in.

## Athlete Development Notes
While not directly a sport science study, understanding how NFL rules evolve—particularly safety-related rule changes (e.g., roughing the passer, crackback blocking)—helps coaching and sports science staff anticipate rule-driven changes to blocking, tackling, and contact techniques that affect player development and practice design in American football.

## Return to Play Notes
Not directly applicable to return-to-play protocols. However, rule evolution around player safety (e.g., defenseless player protections, targeting rules) influences the context in which head and contact injuries occur, which indirectly shapes RTP considerations at the professional level.
