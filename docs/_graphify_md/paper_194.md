# Palmieri-Smith RM, Mack CD, Brophy RH, Owens BD, Herzog MM, Beynnon BD, Spindler KP, Wojtys EM. Epidemiology of anterior cruciate ligament tears in the National Football League. Am J Sports Med. 2021. DOI: 10.1177/03635465211010146

## TLDR
NFL ACL injuries average 62 per year, with the highest risk during preseason games and special teams play. Less experienced players (≤3 years) account for 75% of tears. These findings suggest targeted prevention efforts should focus on early-career players, the preseason period, and special teams units.

## Abstract
This retrospective descriptive epidemiology study examined all ACL injuries entered into the NFL leaguewide electronic health record system from the 2015–2019 seasons (n = 16,403 eligible player-seasons). A total of 314 ACL injuries occurred over 5 years (mean 62/year). The overall 1-season injury risk was 1.9% (95% CI 1.7%–2.1%). Most tears were complete (90%) and nearly all complete tears were treated with reconstruction. The majority of injuries occurred during games (63%) and the preseason (40%). Special teams players had the highest game injury rate (7.6 per 10,000 player-plays), and punt plays had the highest play-type rate (2.19 per 1000 plays). Players with ≤3 years of NFL experience sustained 75% of all tears. Preseason game rates (6.1) were more than double regular season rates (2.7) per 10,000 player-plays.

## Methods
Retrospective descriptive epidemiology study using the NFL's centralized leaguewide electronic health record (EHR) for seasons 2015–2019. Incidence was calculated as 1-season injury risk (exact binomial distribution) and rates per 10,000 player-plays (Poisson regression). Covariates included injured side, severity, treatment, timing, injury setting, play type, and player demographics including years of NFL service.

## Findings
314 ACL injuries occurred over 5 seasons (mean 62/year); 1-season risk 1.9%. Right and left knee equally affected. 90% were complete tears; 100% of complete tears treated with ACL reconstruction. 63% of injuries occurred in games; preseason game rate (6.1/10,000 player-plays) was 2.3x higher than regular season (2.7). Special teams players had the highest game rate (7.6/10,000 player-plays); punt plays the highest play-type rate (2.19/1000 plays). Players with ≤3 years of experience accounted for 75% of injuries. 35% of all ACL injuries occurred during training camp through preseason week 2.

## Limitations
Pre-NFL ACL injury history not consistently captured, precluding recurrence analysis. Practice participation/exposure data unavailable, preventing practice incidence rate calculations. Weight, height, and BMI recorded at time of signing, not at injury. Partial vs. complete designation made by team medical staff without mandatory imaging confirmation.

## Practical Implications
Prevention programs targeting ACL injury should prioritize early-career NFL players and the preseason period. Special teams units—particularly punt coverage—warrant specific attention given their disproportionately high injury rates. Early identification of anatomic risk factors and individualized conditioning progressions during training camp may reduce risk. Rule changes or practice modifications during the first two weeks of preseason could have the greatest population-level impact.

## Athlete Development Notes
Younger players (rookies and 1-year veterans) are at highest ACL risk, suggesting that the transition to elite NFL-level practice and game intensity is a critical vulnerability window. Progressive loading protocols, strength and neuromuscular training targeting knee stability, and monitored preseason ramp-up plans are particularly important for players entering their first three NFL seasons.

## Return to Play Notes
Approximately two-thirds of NFL players return to play following ACL reconstruction, though many perform at a lower level than pre-injury. Complete tears were universally treated with reconstruction. Return-to-play programming should account for position-specific demands, with special teams players potentially requiring additional sport-specific preparation given the change-of-direction and collision loads involved.
