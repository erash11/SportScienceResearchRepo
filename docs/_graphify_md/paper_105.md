# Nwachukwu BU, Bedi A, Premkumar A, Draovitch P, Kelly BT. Characteristics and Outcomes of Arthroscopic Femoroacetabular Impingement Surgery in the National Football League. Am J Sports Med. 2017. doi:10.1177/0363546517729163

## TLDR
NFL players who undergo arthroscopic FAI surgery have a very high RTP rate of 92.5% at a mean of 6 months—faster and higher than most other orthopedic procedures in professional football. Performance metrics showed small, non-significant declines consistent with natural career progression in matched controls, and mean annual salary was not adversely affected. For football performance and medical staff, this study provides strong evidence that symptomatic FAI with labral pathology should not be viewed as a career-ending condition; prompt surgical management when conservative care fails leads to favorable return-to-competition outcomes.

## Abstract
This cohort study investigated the outcomes of arthroscopic surgery for symptomatic femoroacetabular impingement (FAI) in NFL players treated at a single institution between 2006 and 2014. Forty-eight hips in 40 NFL players (mean age 25.6 years) were included; all hips had labral tears, 85.4% underwent labral repair, and 87.5% had cam decompression. Return-to-play (RTP) and performance metrics (offensive power rating and defensive power rating) were assessed pre- and postoperatively and compared to a matched control cohort. Of 40 players, 37 (92.5%) returned to NFL competition at a mean of 6.0 months post-surgery with no statistically significant decline in performance or earnings.

## Methods
Cohort study (Level of evidence 3) of NFL players who underwent arthroscopic FAI surgery by a single surgeon at the Hospital for Special Surgery between 2006 and 2014. Medical records reviewed for demographics, clinical, and operative variables. RTP defined as participation in any regular-season NFL game. Offensive power rating (OPR = [total yards/10] + [total touchdowns x 6]) and defensive power rating (DPR = total tackles + [sacks x 2] + [interceptions x 2]) calculated from publicly available statistics. A matched control cohort (1:1, by position and age ±2 years) from the 2008–2009 NFL roster was used to contextualize performance trends. Paired t-tests compared pre- and post-injury season performance.

## Findings
Forty-eight hips in 40 NFL players from 17 teams included. All hips had labral tears; cam-type impingement most prevalent (87.5%). Eight players (20%) had bilateral FAI. RTP achieved by 37 of 40 players (92.5%) at a mean 6.0 months (range 3–11 months). Pre- to post-injury games played (11.0 vs 9.5, P=.26), OPR (40.2 vs 32.3, P=.34), and DPR (49.6 vs 36.4, P=.10) all showed non-significant declines similar to the control group's natural career progression. Players averaged 3.3 seasons post-surgery (unilateral) vs 2.5 for controls (P=.47). Mean annual salary increased from $3.3M pre-injury to $3.6M post-surgery (P=.58). Offensive linemen were the most common position (27.5%).

## Limitations
Single-surgeon series at a tertiary referral center limits generalizability to other surgeons and institutions. Patient-reported outcome measures were not collected; performance-based proxies (OPR/DPR) used instead. Only the first post-injury season analyzed for performance; later seasons subject to confounders. RTP determined by first regular-season game appearance, potentially underestimating earlier physical readiness. Players with bilateral procedures may have different trajectories than unilateral cases.

## Practical Implications
Football medical and performance staffs should recognize that FAI with labral pathology is common across offensive and defensive NFL positions, with offensive linemen most commonly affected. Approximately 1 in 5 players will have bilateral pathology and should be screened at the time of the index surgery to allow proactive planning. When conservative management fails, arthroscopic FAI surgery offers a 92.5% RTP rate and approximately 6-month recovery. Pre-season hip screening and appropriate imaging (alpha angle assessment) may help identify at-risk athletes before they become symptomatic.

## Athlete Development Notes
Evaluation of hip morphology at the NFL Combine and during pre-participation physicals is warranted given the high prevalence of FAI morphology in football players. Athletes with elevated alpha angles and hip/groin symptoms should be referred for hip preservation consultation. Hip mobility training and core stability work may help offset functional limitations imposed by cam or pincer morphology. Coaches should be aware that hip/groin symptoms in linemen and contact-position players may signal FAI rather than simple muscle strains, warranting imaging rather than only conservative rehabilitation.

## Return to Play Notes
A 6-month RTP timeline after arthroscopic FAI surgery is realistic for NFL players with appropriate surgical correction, labral repair, and structured rehabilitation. Players returned to similar games played and comparable performance ratings, suggesting full functional recovery is achievable. Bilateral cases require staged procedures with approximately 7 months between surgeries. Rehabilitation should emphasize hip ROM restoration, progressive loading of hip stabilizers, and sport-specific movement preparation before return to full contact. No players required revision surgery in this series.
