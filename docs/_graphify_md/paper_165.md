# Benson EM, Elphingstone JW, Paul KD, Schick S, Shihab YA, Barlow D, Ponce BA, Brabston EW, Momaya AM. Eccentric Hamstring Strength Imbalance among Football and Soccer Athletes. South Med J. 2024;117(4):214-219. doi: 10.14423/SMJ.0000000000001674

## TLDR
High school soccer athletes show substantially greater interlimb eccentric hamstring imbalances than football athletes, placing them at elevated risk for hamstring strain injury. Imbalances improve with age and competitive level in soccer (likely reflecting more structured eccentric training), but football athletes show no significant change across skill levels. Single-sport specialization in high school is associated with greater work imbalances than multisport participation. Football programs that already emphasize weight training may be inadvertently protecting players from hamstring asymmetry.

## Abstract
This cross-sectional study compared eccentric hamstring strength imbalances across 631 soccer and American football athletes at high school, collegiate, and professional levels using the NordBord Hamstring Testing System. The study measured maximum hamstring force, torque, and work imbalances between limbs. High school soccer athletes demonstrated significantly greater hamstring imbalances than football athletes, and imbalances decreased with advancing skill level in soccer but not in football.

## Methods
Cross-sectional study enrolling 631 athletes during preseason 2021, including high school (n=459), collegiate (n=149), and professional (n=24) male soccer and football players plus female soccer athletes. Nordic hamstring exercise was performed on the NordBord device. Maximum force (N), torque (N·m), and work (N·s) imbalances between legs were computed. One-way ANOVA with LSD post-hoc tests and chi-square tests compared groups; statistical significance set at P<0.05.

## Findings
High school soccer players showed significantly greater torque imbalance (P=0.03) and work imbalance (P<0.01) than football players. Male soccer athletes at high school had significantly higher maximum force (P=0.018), torque (P=0.035), and work (P=0.033) imbalances than college/professional counterparts. Female high school soccer players had significantly higher torque (P=0.045) and work (P=0.001) imbalances than collegiate females. Football athletes showed no significant imbalance changes across skill levels. Single-sport high school athletes had significantly greater work imbalances than multisport athletes (9.1% vs 7.0%, P=0.012). HSI rates were higher in soccer (11.7%) than football (4.5%, P=0.004).

## Limitations
Pre-measurement activity was not controlled. Imbalances were not stratified by field position due to insufficient statistical power. Cross-sectional design prevents causal conclusions about whether improved balance results from skill-level advancement or self-selection. Study was conducted at limited institutions and may not generalize broadly.

## Practical Implications
Football strength and conditioning staff should incorporate eccentric hamstring testing (NordBord or similar) as a screening tool, particularly for high school athletes and positions requiring explosive acceleration/deceleration (DBs, WRs). Force imbalances exceeding 20% should trigger targeted single-leg eccentric programming. Nordic curl protocols have demonstrated 50-70% reduction in initial HSI risk and 85% reduction in reinjury rates and should be considered standard injury prevention for all skill-position players.

## Athlete Development Notes
Early enrollment of football players in structured weight training — already common in most programs — appears protective against eccentric hamstring imbalance. Multisport participation in high school is associated with better hamstring balance and should be encouraged where possible. For positions with high sprint demands (DBs, WRs, RBs), single-leg deadlifts, Nordic curls, and single-leg Romanian chair holds should be integrated into year-round strength programs to address asymmetry proactively.

## Return to Play Notes
Athletes returning from hamstring strain injury should be cleared using NordBord testing to confirm limb symmetry before full sport return. Work imbalance >20% between legs indicates elevated re-injury risk (reported recurrence rates of 14-34%). A progressive eccentric loading protocol — including Nordic curls — should be completed before RTP clearance, given the 85% reinjury risk reduction demonstrated with this training approach.
