# Morvant BA, Heintz EC, Foreman JJ. Effects of Age and Travel on National Football League Quarterback Performance: A Correlational Study. International Journal of Kinesiology & Sports Science. 2021;9(1):45-50. 10.7575/aiac.ijkss.v.9n.1p.45

## TLDR
NFL travel distance negatively affects quarterback performance, but this effect is age-dependent: younger quarterbacks (e.g., 23-year-olds) show the sharpest rating drops after long road trips, while older quarterbacks (mid-30s and beyond) actually tend to perform better after long-distance travel, possibly due to more developed travel-management strategies and higher cognitive experience. Teams and coaches should account for quarterback age when planning travel logistics and managing preparation windows before away games.

## Abstract
This correlational study examined the effects of age, travel distance, and their interaction on NFL quarterback passer ratings using data from the 2018 season (N=527 quarterback-game observations). OLS regression was used to predict quarterback ratings from age, distance traveled, season week, weeks since bye week, and an age-distance interaction term. Results showed that season week, weeks since bye week, and distance traveled all had significant negative effects on quarterback rating, while the age-performance relationship was nonlinear and the impact of travel differed by quarterback age.

## Methods
Correlational research design using publicly available data from pro-football-reference.com. Sample: 527 quarterback-game observations from the 2018 NFL season. Variables: quarterback rating (dependent), age, age-squared, distance traveled (miles), weeks since bye week, season week, and an age-distance interaction. Ordinary least squares (OLS) regression analyzed both the full season (including playoffs, n=527) and regular season only (n=503).

## Findings
Season week (p=0.04), weeks since bye week (p=0.041), and distance traveled (p=0.031) had significant negative effects on quarterback rating. The age-performance relationship was nonlinear (p=0.018): ratings declined through an athlete's 20s then increased through the 30s and 40s. The age-distance interaction (p=0.072) showed that younger QBs' ratings decreased with farther travel while older QBs' ratings increased. For example, 23-year-old Baker Mayfield dropped from 100.1 to 70.7 after traveling ~2,500 miles, while 36-year-old Ben Roethlisberger improved from 96.26 to 130.2 over the same distance.

## Limitations
Data from only one NFL season (2018) limits generalizability. Only quarterback position was examined, precluding insights for other positions. Direction of travel (east vs. west) was not included, which prior research suggests matters. Causal mechanisms underlying the age-travel interaction are speculative and cannot be confirmed with this correlational design.

## Practical Implications
NFL coaching and performance staffs should factor quarterback age into travel preparation protocols. Younger quarterbacks may benefit most from structured jet-lag mitigation routines (optimized hydration, sleep scheduling, light exposure management). Bye week timing meaningfully affects performance in subsequent weeks, reinforcing the value of maximizing recovery during bye weeks. Scheduling departments should consider age-travel dynamics when planning road game preparation timelines.

## Athlete Development Notes
The non-linear age-performance curve observed for quarterbacks — declining through the 20s before rebounding in the 30s — suggests cognitive development and experience accumulation may offset physical decline at the QB position. Younger developing quarterbacks should be given structured education and experience with long-distance travel management as part of their professional development curriculum.

## Return to Play Notes
The negative effect of accumulated season weeks and time since bye week on QB performance highlights how cumulative fatigue compounds over a season. For return-to-play planning, the timing of a player's re-entry relative to recent travel demands and proximity to the last bye week should be factored into readiness assessments, particularly for cognitively demanding skill positions.
