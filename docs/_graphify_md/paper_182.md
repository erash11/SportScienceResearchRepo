# Moreno-Pérez V, Del Coso J, Romero-Rodríguez D, Marcé-Hernández L, Peñaranda M, Madruga-Parera M. Effects of home confinement due to COVID-19 pandemic on eccentric hamstring muscle strength in football players. Scandinavian Journal of Medicine & Science in Sports. 2020;30:2010-2012. 10.1111/sms.13768

## TLDR
Even when semi-professional soccer players followed prescribed home training programs during COVID-19 confinement, eccentric hamstring strength declined by 8% after 28 days and by 14.1% after 49 days of isolation. This is the first study to quantify detraining-related hamstring weakness during confinement and provides a clear warning for football staff: extended periods away from sport-specific training — including sprinting, acceleration, and deceleration — substantially increase hamstring injury risk upon return. The authors recommend a structured transition period before return to official competition, calibrated to the length of confinement.

## Abstract
This prospective observational study measured eccentric hamstring muscle strength at three timepoints (14, 28, and 49 days) during COVID-19 home confinement in 30 semi-professional football (soccer) players who had been confined since March 14, 2020. Strength was assessed using the Nordic hamstring exercise and a validated video-based smartphone method. Despite players following prescribed home training programs from their teams' S&C staff, eccentric hamstring strength declined significantly and progressively throughout confinement.

## Methods
Prospective observational letter study with 30 semi-professional football (soccer) players from a Spanish Soccer League team. Confinement began March 14, 2020 for 7 weeks. Players followed prescribed home programs including strength training with bodyweight loads, proprioception, and high-intensity intermittent routines. Eccentric hamstring strength measured using the Nordic hamstring exercise at 14, 28, and 49 days post-confinement onset. A validated video-based smartphone method (Nordics App) was used to calculate Nordic break-point angle and hamstring force. Measurements taken at the same time of day following a rest day. Test-retest reliability: CV = 1.2±3.2% for break-point angle.

## Findings
Bilateral Nordic break-point angle decreased by 1.9% at 28 days and by 4.4% at 49 days compared to 14-day values (p<0.05). These angular reductions corresponded to eccentric hamstring strength decreases of 8.0% at 28 days and 14.1% at 49 days of confinement (p<0.01). Similar patterns were observed in both dominant and non-dominant limbs. Reductions occurred despite players maintaining prescribed home training programs targeting lower limb strength, suggesting body-weight-only training is insufficient to maintain football-specific hamstring strength.

## Limitations
Letter to the editor with a small sample (n=30) from a single semi-professional team; findings may not fully generalize to professional or elite players. The comparison reference is the 14-day timepoint rather than pre-confinement baseline, which may underestimate total decline if strength was already decreasing in the first two weeks. Only eccentric hamstring strength was measured; other muscle groups (quadriceps, hip flexors, calf) were not quantified. No control group of non-confined players was available.

## Practical Implications
Football performance and medical staff must build a structured return-to-play loading period between the end of any prolonged off-site or virtual training phase and the onset of full-contact practice. Given that 14.1% eccentric hamstring strength loss accumulated over just 7 weeks of home training, teams returning from extended absences should incorporate progressive Nordic hamstring exercise volume, on-field sprint progressions, and acceleration/deceleration work over a minimum 2-4 week bridge period before resuming full competitive load.

## Athlete Development Notes
The study confirms that football-specific physical qualities — particularly the eccentric hamstring strength required during sprinting — cannot be maintained through bodyweight home programs alone. This has direct implications for NFL offseason programming: extended virtual-only or limited-facility periods should be supplemented with accessible eccentric hamstring loading tools (e.g., portable Nordic benches, resistance bands for hamstring curls). Athletes should be individually screened upon return with the Nordic hamstring test or equivalent eccentric assessment before full sprint-based practice is cleared.

## Return to Play Notes
A 14.1% reduction in eccentric hamstring strength after 7 weeks of limited training represents a clinically meaningful increase in hamstring strain injury risk. Return-to-play protocols following any extended training disruption should include a mandatory eccentric hamstring strength assessment baseline and a graded re-loading program before high-speed sprint work is reintroduced. This is particularly critical for positions with high sprint demands (wide receivers, cornerbacks, running backs, safeties) and for athletes with a prior hamstring injury history.
