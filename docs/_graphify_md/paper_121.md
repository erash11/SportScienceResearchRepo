# Roach MA, Owens MF. Competitive Position, Expectations, and Managerial Approaches to Risk in National Football League Football. J Sport Manag. 2025;39(5):342-353. https://doi.org/10.1123/jsm.2024-0254

## TLDR
NFL coaches take more risk on fourth down when their win probability is low and when they have experienced high in-game win probabilities earlier in the game, consistent with prospect theory loss-frame behavior. Despite this increased aggressiveness, teams still systematically attempt too few fourth-down plays relative to the win-probability-maximizing threshold, representing a persistent coaching inefficiency. Being a pregame betting favorite and having a high prior in-game max win probability both independently increase fourth-down aggressiveness. Football operations and analytics staff should use expected-value frameworks to guide fourth-down decisions, as increased aggressiveness in these situations—even in non-optimal subsets—improves expected win outcomes by approximately 1.56 percentage points per attempt.

## Abstract
This study analyzed how NFL coaches incorporate competitive position and win expectations into fourth-down decision-making using play-by-play data from 2008-2018 NFL regular seasons. Probit regression models estimated the likelihood of attempting a fourth-down conversion as a function of current win probability, pregame betting line, and maximum in-game win probability, while controlling for game situation variables, coach experience, and season fixed effects. The study tested predictions from prospect theory and rank-order tournament theory, finding that teams in weaker competitive positions and those with higher prior in-game success were systematically more aggressive on fourth down.

## Methods
Play-by-play data from 2008-2018 NFL regular seasons obtained from sports-reference.com (37,692 total regular season fourth downs; primary analytical subset of 18,366 plays where attempting a conversion offered a greater expected change in win probability than punting or a field goal). Three expected-change-in-win-probability models estimated the optimal fourth-down decision for each play. Probit regression models estimated probability of a fourth-down attempt as a function of win probability, betting line, max win probability, and their interactions, controlling for field position, yards to go, game quarter, lead, home team, division rival, neutral site, game week, and coach experience. OLS regression estimated actual change in win probability from fourth-down attempts.

## Findings
Teams attempted fourth-down conversions 12.2% of the time in the primary subset, converting 64.6% of attempts. A 10-percentage-point increase in win probability reduced the likelihood of attempting a fourth down by 2.67 percentage points in the optimal-play subset (Specification 4). Being favored by one additional betting-line point increased fourth-down attempt likelihood by approximately 0.26 percentage points. A 10-percentage-point increase in maximum in-game win probability increased the likelihood of attempting a fourth-down play by 0.54 percentage points in the optimal subset. Teams in the 0-25% win probability bin attempted plays 18.2% of the time vs 8.2% for teams in the 75-100% bin. Fourth-down attempts generated an average expected change in win probability of +1.56 percentage points relative to punting or a field goal across all situations, confirming persistent under-aggressiveness. Interactions between win probability and betting line, and win probability and max win probability, confirmed that reference point effects are concentrated in low win probability situations.

## Limitations
Win probability data from sports-reference.com were unavailable after 2018, limiting the sample to a period before the wider adoption of analytics-driven fourth-down culture. The study cannot separate rational quality-differential explanations from behavioral loss-aversion explanations for the betting-line effects. Coach-level motivations and individual decision processes are unobservable. Reference points are theoretical constructs not directly measured. Results may reflect changing league norms during the study period.

## Practical Implications
Football operations and coaching staffs should implement data-driven fourth-down decision frameworks that use expected win probability as the primary decision criterion. The persistent tendency to punt or kick when analytics favor going for it represents a systematic and exploitable inefficiency. Understanding that teams in desperate positions are more aggressive on fourth down can inform defensive preparation and opponent game-planning. Organizations should track their own fourth-down decision accuracy over time to identify systematic biases in their coaching staff.

## Athlete Development Notes
While primarily a sport management and economics paper, findings have indirect implications for game planning and situational football preparation. Understanding that fourth-down conversion attempts succeed 64.6% of the time in optimal situations informs offensive and defensive coordinators about practice priorities in short-yardage, goal-line, and fourth-down scenarios. Investing developmental resources in these situational packages may yield compounding win-probability benefits.

## Return to Play Notes
This paper does not address return-to-play topics. Its focus is on in-game managerial risk decisions and coaching behavior, not player health or injury recovery.
