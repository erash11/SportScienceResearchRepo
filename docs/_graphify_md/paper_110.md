# Smith RS, Guilleminault C, Efron B. Circadian Rhythms and Enhanced Athletic Performance in the National Football League. Sleep. 1997;20(5):362-365.

## TLDR
West Coast NFL teams hold a significant circadian performance advantage over East Coast teams during Monday Night Football, winning 63.5% of games and outperforming Las Vegas point spread predictions in 67.9% of cases. This advantage is comparable to or greater than home-field advantage in magnitude. EC teams playing at 9:00 p.m. EST are performing near their biological nadir, while WC teams are competing around their physiological peak (6:00 p.m. local time). Football staff should treat game time relative to home time zone as a meaningful performance variable, not just travel logistics.

## Abstract
This retrospective study examined 25 years (1970–1994) of Monday Night Football (MNF) games between West Coast (WC) and East Coast (EC) NFL teams to test the hypothesis that circadian timing confers an athletic advantage. WC teams playing at 9:00 p.m. EST are effectively competing at 6:00 p.m. their local time (closer to peak performance), while EC teams play near their biological nadir. Logistic regression controlling for point spreads and home-field advantage showed that WC teams won significantly more often and by more points than EC teams, outperforming Las Vegas point spread predictions by a statistically significant margin.

## Methods
Retrospective data analysis of all 63 MNF games involving EC versus WC NFL teams from the 1970–1994 seasons (25 seasons) with 9:00 p.m. EST start times. EC teams defined as those in the Eastern Standard time zone; WC teams as those in the Pacific time zone. Win-loss records, point margins, and performance relative to Las Vegas point spreads were examined using logistic regression with home-field advantage as an indicator variable. Two logistic regressions were run: one with home games as the indicator and one also including point spread as a predictor. All-time home and away winning percentages since 1970 were calculated and compared to MNF performance.

## Findings
WC teams won 63.5% of games versus EC teams' 36.5%, winning by an average of 14.7 points per game compared to EC's 9.0 points. WC teams outperformed Las Vegas point spread predictions at p<0.01 in both logistic regressions. Simply picking the WC team predicted the winner against the spread 67.9% of the time. WC teams' MNF home winning % was 71% (vs. their 59.3% all-time home rate); EC teams' MNF home winning % was 43.8% (vs. their 56.5% all-time). Overall record difference between WC and EC was only 4.4 percentage points, far less than the 27-point MNF gap.

## Limitations
Retrospective study unable to determine exact circadian phase of each athlete at game time. Travel strategies of teams changed over the 25-year study window. Individual variation in circadian adjustment to time zone changes could not be assessed. Point spread data from 1970–1972 (7 games) could not be located and were excluded from the point-spread regression. Confounding variables, though largely nullified by the point spread analysis, could not be completely eliminated.

## Practical Implications
NFL staff should treat game time relative to home time zone as a critical performance variable. EC teams playing MNF against WC opponents should consider phase-delaying circadian clocks by 3 hours in the days prior to the game. WC teams should maintain home-zone scheduling when traveling east. The circadian effect appears at least as impactful as home-field advantage, suggesting sleep and circadian management is a high-ROI intervention for performance staff.

## Athlete Development Notes
Circadian rhythm education should be integrated into athlete development programs. Training times influence biological peak performance timing, and athletes who consistently train in the late afternoon may benefit from scheduling practices aligned with anticipated game times. For developing players, establishing consistent sleep-wake schedules tied to competition times can optimize performance readiness over the long season.

## Return to Play Notes
Circadian considerations are relevant when scheduling return-to-play progression for injured athletes. Rehabilitation sessions and functional testing should ideally be conducted at the same time of day as typical game times to ensure assessments reflect true game-ready performance. Athletes returning from injury during road trips should have travel-related circadian disruption factored into clearance decisions.
