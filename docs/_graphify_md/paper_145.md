# Kuhn AW, Coughlin MJ, McGonegle SJ, Herzog MM, Weir N, O'Cain CM, Anderson RB. Distal Tibiofibular Syndesmosis Injuries in the National Football League (NFL): A Spectrum of Pathology That Correlates With Time to Return to Full Participation. Sports Health. 2024. doi:10.1177/19417381241253223

## TLDR
High ankle sprains in the NFL are rarely isolated: only 13% of syndesmosis injuries occur without concomitant pathology, and most involve deltoid ligament or lateral ligament complex injury. What matters most for return-to-play timing is not the MRI grade of the syndesmosis itself, but the presence of an associated fracture or complete deep deltoid ligament tear/diastasis. Three injury clusters drive prognosis: fracture combinations (~250 days), complete deltoid tear or diastasis (~175 days), and all other syndesmosis injuries (~27 days). Staff and clinicians should look beyond the 'high ankle sprain' label and classify injury severity by concomitant findings to set accurate return timelines.

## Abstract
This retrospective cross-sectional study evaluated 83 external rotation ankle injuries involving the distal tibiofibular syndesmosis in NFL players from the 2017–2019 seasons. All injuries were identified through the NFL Injury Surveillance Database, confirmed by video review, and subjected to comprehensive MRI review by a multidisciplinary team. Stepwise linear regression was used to identify MRI variables that predicted time to return to full participation.

## Methods
Retrospective cross-sectional study. NFL Injury Surveillance Database queried for external rotation ankle injuries (2017–2019 pre-, regular-, and postseasons). Injuries confirmed by NFL Vision video review. MRI and radiograph/CT required within 30 days of injury onset. MRI reviewed by a musculoskeletal radiologist and two orthopaedic surgeons assessing AITFL, PITFL, IOM, deltoid ligament, lateral ligament complex, tendon pathology, osteochondral lesions, bone contusions, fractures, and diastasis. Stepwise linear regression (forward, backward) identified imaging predictors of days to return to full participation. 108 injuries met full imaging inclusion criteria; 83 classified as syndesmosis injuries.

## Findings
83 syndesmosis injuries analyzed. Isolated syndesmosis injuries (n=11, 13%) were uncommon; most were combined with other ligamentous injury (n=65, 78%) or fractures (n=7, 8%). Overall median time to return to full participation was 30 days (IQR 19–73). Three injury groups emerged from regression: Group 1 (fracture combinations): median 250 days (IQR 142–266); Group 2 (complete deep deltoid tear or acute diastasis): median 175 days (IQR 20–248); Group 3 (all other syndesmosis injuries): median 27 days (IQR 18–46). One-way ANOVA confirmed significant between-group differences (p<0.01). Fractures required surgery 71% of the time; sprain combinations 17%; isolated injuries 9%.

## Limitations
MRI reviewed in tandem rather than independently, potentially introducing bias. Wide variability in MRI scanner strength and protocols across sites. Data limited to 2017–2019 seasons and game injuries only (no practice injuries). Study excluded players without adequate video confirmation of injury mechanism. Numerous factors affecting RTP beyond MRI were not modeled. Results are specific to elite NFL athletes and may not generalize broadly.

## Practical Implications
NFL athletic trainers and team physicians should move beyond diagnosing 'high ankle sprains' as a single entity. MRI classification identifying associated fractures or complete deep deltoid tears/diastasis should directly inform return-to-play timelines—these athletes face 6–8+ month recoveries and are likely to require surgical intervention. The vast majority of syndesmosis injuries without these findings resolve in approximately 3–4 weeks. Accurate early imaging review should drive individualized communication with coaches and front offices about realistic availability windows.

## Athlete Development Notes
Distal tibiofibular syndesmosis injuries in football carry a wide spectrum of severity with dramatically different timelines. Ankle injury rehabilitation and return-to-performance progressions should be risk-stratified by MRI findings rather than a blanket 'high ankle sprain' protocol. Athletes with concomitant deltoid involvement or diastasis require substantially longer timelines and should not be rushed through progressive loading progressions.

## Return to Play Notes
Syndesmosis injuries without fracture or complete deltoid tear have a median RTP of ~27 days and can be managed nonoperatively in most cases. Complete deep deltoid tears or acute diastasis extend median RTP to ~175 days and frequently require surgery. Syndesmosis-fracture combinations (Weber C, Maisonneuve) carry the longest recovery at ~250 days with 71% requiring surgical fixation. Early MRI with comprehensive ligamentous and fracture review is essential for accurate RTP prognosis in NFL players with external rotation ankle injuries.
